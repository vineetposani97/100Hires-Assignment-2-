## Post 1: Business is simpler than you think

Link - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/justinwelsh_youre-overcomplicating-business-trust-activity-7434082901404762112-Uhgb?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAABCd18UBZhw_DC1Kh6aT2qHRjLe9bqO1Ej4

**Key Idea:**
You don’t need a complex business plan. Start simple and validate with real people.

**Content:**
You’re overcomplicating business.

Trust me.

You don’t need some monster business plan to be successful, but if you want one, here it is:

1. Pick one problem you can solve
2. Find one person who has it
3. Charge them to fix it

That’s the whole thing. Three steps.

You don’t need to set up an LLC.
You don’t need a logo at all.
You don’t need a strategy doc.
You don’t even need a domain name.

You only need one person to say, "Yes, I’ll pay you for that."

Everything else in your business comes after that moment. Not before.

**Why this matters:**
- Focuses on revenue first
- Avoids overthinking
- Encourages action over planning

## Post 2: The real entrepreneurial journey

Link - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/justinwelsh_you-cant-sprint-to-entrepreneurial-success-activity-7433855654320095232-e23k?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAABCd18UBZhw_DC1Kh6aT2qHRjLe9bqO1Ej4

**Key Idea:**
Success is a long-term process with phases, not quick wins.

**Content:**
You can’t sprint to entrepreneurial success.

It’s a "marathon style" game.

Here’s what the real journey looks like:

1. Phase 1 is survival
2. Phase 2 is building systems
3. Phase 3 is investing in growth
4. Phase 4 is setting up your defense
5. Phase 5 is automating the business

This journey takes years of learning, failing, and improving.

**Why this matters:**
- Sets realistic expectations
- Teaches long-term thinking
- Breaks growth into clear stages

## Post 3: Luck comes from effort

**Key Idea:**
Luck is created through consistent action and effort.

**Content:**
The more you do, the luckier you get.

The "luckiest" people:

In the end, luck is just the total time spent learning + the number of good things you put into the world.

**Why this matters:**
